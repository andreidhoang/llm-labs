### softcap=15 is LOAD-BEARING (iter5)

```python
# Original (core/model.py:GPT.forward):
softcap = 15
logits = softcap * torch.tanh(logits / softcap)

# Relaxed to 30 in iter5 — val_bpb went 1.058 → 1.062 (+0.005)
```

**Implication:** the tanh smoothing of logits matters. Relaxing the cap by 2x
hurts. **Recommended:** keep softcap=15 in Tier 2.

## Two findings of "minimal effect" (iter2, iter4) — also useful

### Init scheme: Normal ≈ Uniform at noise floor (iter2)

Standard Uniform(-s, s) with `s = √3/√n_embd` and Normal(0, 1/√n_embd) gave
val_bpb diff of −0.0007 — within noise. Both at d=8.

**Implication:** Karpathy's preference for Uniform "to avoid outliers" doesn't
appear load-bearing at d=8. Could simplify to Normal everywhere. Marginal.

### RoPE base: 10K → 100K marginal at seq=2048 (iter4)

Llama/Qwen use 100K. nanochat uses 10K. We tested at our seq_len=2048 and got
−0.0003 (essentially noise). **Reason:** RoPE base mostly matters for seq_len
much greater than base/(2π) ≈ 1591. At 2048 we're barely past that threshold.

**Implication:** at our 2048 context, RoPE base is not load-bearing. Would
likely matter at seq=4096+ (Tier 2 may or may not test this).
