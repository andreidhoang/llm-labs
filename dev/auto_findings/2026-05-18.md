## Tier 2 promotion candidates

### → H₃ (Bergsma "Power Lines": B ∝ D^0.383) — at d=8, 2^19 overshoots

Iter3 result: TOTAL_BATCH_SIZE 2^18 → 2^19 regressed by +0.0375 val_bpb. At d=8,
n_embd=512, the Bergsma scaling rule predicts B_opt ∝ D^0.383. Doubling beyond
this optimum produces fewer optimizer steps in the fixed budget, and the smoother
gradients can't compensate.

**Recommended action for Tier 2:** verify B = B_opt(d=24) for sweep_design.md's
d=24 cells matches Bergsma's prediction; expect closer alignment at larger D since
the rule was calibrated higher. Consider a mini batch-size cell ($32) if any cell
shows training-noise concern.

### → H₄ (sweep_design.md, cell E5: dense vs MoE) — STRENGTHENED

Session 1 found dense beats MoE-on by 4.3% at d=8. Session 2 iter1 REPLICATED this
exactly: val_bpb 1.062 (dense+tuned) vs 1.105 (MoE-on track from session 1).
Cross-session reproducibility = strong evidence. Tier 2's E5 (dense G=1) cell
should win wall-clock-to-T.

### → Negative finding worth noting: WARMUP_RATIO=0 hurts at d=8

Karpathy's autoresearch defaults use WARMUP_RATIO=0 (immediate full LR). At d=8
we measured +0.0253 val_bpb regression. The 15s of saved warmup is dominated by
optimizer instability at full LR step 1 with freshly-initialized weights.

**Recommended action for Tier 2:** preserve WARMUP_RATIO≥0.05 across all cells.
Karpathy's choice was calibrated for his larger Karpathy-scale defaults; doesn't
transfer cleanly down.
